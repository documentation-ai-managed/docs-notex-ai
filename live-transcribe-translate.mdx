## **1. What is this feature for?**

- **International Meetings:** Understand foreign partners immediately without needing a live interpreter.

- **Lectures & Seminars:** Capture every important detail without worrying about missing a word.

- **Interviews & Research:** Automate your transcription process and save hours of manual work.

## **2. Step-by-Step Guide**

### **Step 1: Get Started**

From the NoteX main dashboard, click the **Record** button (the blue icon in the left-hand menu) to open the recording control panel.

### **Step 2: Configure Your Settings**

In the **Record Audio** window, check the following options:

- **Live Transcription:** Toggle the switch to the right (it will turn blue) to enable real-time scrolling text.

- **Translation Language:** Select the language you want the text translated into (e.g., English or Vietnamese).

- **Speech Language:** Select the source language currently being spoken.

- **Folder:** Choose where you want to save your recording.

- **Audio Settings:** Ensure your microphone is connected and receiving a signal.

### **Step 3: Record and Monitor**

Click **Start Record**. The screen will split into two sections:

- **Left Side:** Displays the original transcript of the speech.

- **Right Side:** Displays the live translation in your chosen language.

### **Step 4: Finish and Save**

While recording, you can click **Pause** or **Reset** to start over. Once finished, click **Create Note** to save the entire transcript and translation to your NoteX account.

## **3. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### Can I choose multiple translation languages at once?

Yes. To ensure the highest accuracy, the system currently supports up to five (5) translation languages per recording session.

### What happens if my microphone disconnects mid-session?

No need to restart! You can go to Audio Settings in the bottom-left corner of the recording screen to re-select your input device and continue.

### What factors affect translation quality?

Accuracy depends on your internet speed, audio volume, and the clarity of the speaker. We recommend using a high-quality microphone for the best results.
